Bad Visualizations

The Composition of Honey

Who is the audience? People who consume honey.

What message and questions does thie visualization convey and answer? What is honey composed of?

What data is encoded in the visualization? The composition of honey broken down into its compounds and their respective quantities.

What tasks do readers perform on the visualization? Readers are able to compare the different quantities of compounds that make up honey.

How are the five principles applied to this visualization?

Why is this visualization bad? First and foremost, the visualization is bad because it is not truthful. The angles and portions of the pie chart are not proportional to the actual quantity that each slice is supposed to represent. For example, even though fructose and sucrose are supposed to make up 38.5% and 37.1% of honey, respectively, the slice that represents fructose makes up almost twice the amount of area that sucrose does. It also does not credit the source that it got its data from so we cannot be sure that this information is credible or not. It is also not very enlightening as it does not really answer any important questions or have any implications of real significance.
